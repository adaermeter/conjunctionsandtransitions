What are Paired Conjunctions?

Paired conjunctions (also called correlative conjunctions) work in pairs to connect words, phrases, or clauses. They must maintain parallel structure in the sentence.

Both...and

Both X and Y

Connects two positive ideas of equal importance.

Examples:

• Both my sister and my brother are doctors.

• She is both intelligent and hardworking.

Either...or

Either X or Y

Presents a choice between two alternatives.

Examples:

• You can have either coffee or tea.

• Either study hard or fail the exam.

Neither...nor

Neither X nor Y

Connects two negative ideas.

Examples:

• Neither John nor Mary came to the party.

• She neither called nor texted me.

Not only...but also

Not only X but also Y

Emphasizes the second element while including the first.

Examples:

• She is not only beautiful but also intelligent.

• He not only sings but also dances well.
